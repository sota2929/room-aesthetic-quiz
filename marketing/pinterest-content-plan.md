# Pinterest Content Plan

## Goal

Use organic Pinterest content to learn which room-aesthetic angles earn saves and clicks, then send interested users to the free **What’s Your Room Aesthetic?** quiz. The first phase is validation, not paid scaling.

## Launch approach

- Start with organic Pins only. Do not use paid ads at launch.
- Link most Pins to the quiz landing page, not directly to a product page.
- Publish 30–50 Pins before drawing broad conclusions.
- Begin with a controlled test of 10 Pins: three quiz invitations, three result teasers, two checklists, one dorm-room Pin, and one small-room makeover Pin.
- Use 2–3 visual templates so the headline and topic—not a completely different layout—are the main variables.
- Review Pinterest Analytics weekly. Compare impressions, saves, outbound clicks, and outbound click rate.
- Keep winning topics and rewrite them with new headlines rather than repeatedly posting identical Pins.

## Core content pillars

1. **Quiz invitations:** Curiosity-led Pins that promise a quick, free result.
2. **Result teasers:** A specific aesthetic and three recognizable traits.
3. **Comparisons:** Two styles side by side to invite self-identification.
4. **Useful mini-guides:** Checklists, color rules, or first-step advice worth saving.
5. **Small-space situations:** Dorm rooms, first apartments, rental-friendly changes, and low-budget resets.
6. **Product teasers:** Light mentions of the Starter Kit only after helpful content has earned attention.

## Keyword bank

- room aesthetic quiz
- bedroom ideas
- dorm room decor
- room makeover

- small bedroom ideas

Use one primary keyword naturally in the Pin title and 2–4 related phrases in the description. Avoid keyword stuffing.

## First 10-Pin test

| Pin IDs | Angle | What it tests |
|---|---|---|
| P001–P003 | Main quiz invitations | Direct quiz vs. dream-room vs. personality framing |
| P011, P016, P021 | Result teasers | Neutral, pastel, and earthy aesthetics |
| P036–P037 | Saveable checklists | Planning and low-budget utility |
| P041 | Dorm-room identity | College-specific relevance |
| P046 | Small-room makeover | Space constraint + personal style |

Post consistently rather than all at once. A simple starting rhythm is one Pin per day for 10 days. The specific posting time matters less than consistency and a clean test.

## Weekly review

- Which headlines earned the highest outbound click rate?
- Which visuals earned saves but few clicks? They may need a clearer CTA.
- Which aesthetic names generated the most interest?
- Did dorm, first-apartment, or general bedroom language perform best?
- Which top two Pins should get 2–3 new variations next week?

Do not introduce paid Pinterest ads until multiple organic Pins repeatedly produce outbound clicks and the quiz-to-product journey is ready.
