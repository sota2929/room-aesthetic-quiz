# Canva Pinterest Design Brief

## Audience and purpose

Create Pinterest Pins that invite U.S. Gen Z women—especially college students, dorm residents, and first-apartment renters—to take a free room-aesthetic quiz. The Pins should feel genuinely useful and aesthetically considered, never corporate or aggressively promotional.

## Format

- Canvas: **1000 × 1500 px**
- Aspect ratio: **2:3 vertical**
- Export: PNG for static Pins
- Keep all essential text comfortably inside the outer 80 px on every side.

## Visual direction

- Mood: soft, cozy, aesthetic, clean, warm, modern
- Palette: warm cream, oatmeal, muted rose, sage, cocoa; use darker espresso for text
- Typography: one expressive editorial serif for short headlines plus one highly legible sans serif for supporting text
- Imagery: cozy bedroom details, dorm-friendly corners, moodboard fragments, color swatches, bedding, lamps, plants, frames, or abstract rounded shapes
- Add subtle paper texture or a soft shadow when helpful; keep contrast high enough for mobile reading.
- Do not imply that a pictured room is the user’s exact result. It is visual inspiration.

## Copy hierarchy

1. A short, readable headline—the first thing visible in the feed
2. One concise subheadline that explains the value
3. A clear CTA such as **Take the free quiz**

Avoid paragraphs, small text, crowded collages, six competing fonts, and low-contrast beige-on-beige copy.

## First three templates

### 1. Quiz invitation Pin

- Large headline in the upper third
- One attractive room image or abstract room moodboard in the center
- Subheadline directly below the headline
- Pill-shaped CTA near the bottom
- Optional six-color strip to hint at possible results

### 2. Result teaser Pin

- Aesthetic name as the headline
- One dominant mood image or clean three-image collage
- Three short trait pills, such as “warm · calm · clutter-free”
- Small line: “Is this your room style?”
- CTA: **Take the free quiz**

### 3. Checklist / room makeover Pin

- Number-led headline, such as “5 Steps Before You Buy More Decor”
- 3–5 large, scannable checklist lines
- Minimal supporting photo or color-block background
- CTA: **Find your room style first**

## Quality check

- Can the headline be read at phone-feed size?
- Is there one obvious focal point?
- Is the CTA visible without feeling like an ad?
- Does the image match the named aesthetic?
- Is the layout still clear with no audio or animation?
- Does the Pin link to the quiz landing page?
